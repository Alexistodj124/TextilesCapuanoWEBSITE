Factory Location: Boca del Monte, Guatemala

Our main textile factory is located in Boca del Monte, Guatemala. Here we produce high-quality yarns and fibers with advanced technology and skilled craftsmanship, serving both local and international markets.

Factory Location: Quetzaltenango (Xela), Guatemala

Our second largest textile factory is located in Quetzaltenango, also known as Xela. This facility specializes in the production of innovative textile blends and supports both domestic and export markets with reliable quality and efficiency.

Factory Location: Amatitlán, Guatemala

Our Amatitlán facility focuses on the production of specialty textile products and supports our distribution network throughout Guatemala. This factory is equipped with modern machinery to ensure consistent quality and efficiency.

Textiles Capuano – Company Timeline

First Generation – 1911
Founded in the city of Quetzaltenango in 1911 by Francisco Capuano, the first textile factory marked the start of a legacy in quality fabric production. With traditional looms and craftsmanship, the company built a reputation for durable materials and attention to detail, becoming a trusted supplier in the region.

Second Generation – 1985
In 1985, the second generation of the Capuano family took over the business, modernizing the production process and expanding the product line. The introduction of new technologies and techniques allowed for greater efficiency and creativity, solidifying the company's position in the market.

Third Generation – 2001
In 2001, the third generation of the Capuano family took the helm, focusing on sustainability and ethical practices. The company embraced eco-friendly materials and processes, setting new standards in the industry and appealing to a more conscious consumer base.
